Who is this for?

Target audience

The target audience is anyone who wants to know the current or future weather. The website will help anyone who wants to get ahead of the climate.

Persona #1

Luke Skywalker

Like any typical millennial, Luke has a high degree of web literacy and often goes online. He is planning to go camping with his father on the planet Tatooine, Darth Vader, next weekend.

He is anxious about the weather and checks every website enthusiastically. He wants a website where it shows more than a current temperature. He wants every website to be very similar to the ones he has previously visited so that it becomes easy to get accustomed to the site in less than a minute.

Persona #2

Darth Manahari

Manahari is now the Sith Lord of the entire galaxy. Darth Manahari wants to conquer the entire universe. However, there is a great disturbance in the force. A young Jedi has become a thorn in Darth Manahari's conquest to dominance.

This is a perfect opportunity for the Sith Lord to show his prowess. The Sith Lord desires all Jedi to be exterminated, so he can rule the entire universe without worrying about pests. Before doing that, he wants to check the weather condition, so he visits lizweathersweather.com.

Any scenarios?

Scenarios

  1. It's 9 a.m, and Luke wants to go to the office. It's gloomy outside. He checks a weather website to make sure if it's going to rain or not.
  2. Ross has a date with Rachel tonight. It's been raining non-stop since yesterday, and he frequently checks the weather website.
  3. Monica has planned a long weekend trip with Chandler. They are planning to go to a Florida beach. They want to make sure the weather is clear all weekend before going.
  4. Joey and his six sisters are going to Yellowstone National Park this Saturday. Joey always makes sure to check the weather before going anywhere.
  5. Phoebe and her husband Mike are going to Italy for their honeymoon. Mike wants a perfect romantic setting, so he frequently checks the best weather that suits the situation.
